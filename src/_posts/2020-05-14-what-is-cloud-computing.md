Welcome to the first post in the series "Explain Like I'm 5", where I will attempt to explain concepts and technologies to a more general audience. If you're trying to learn about cloud concepts and why various AWS services exist this is the series for you. In this first post we will lay the foundations with explaining what the modern cloud actually is.

<!--more-->

Before we talk about what Cloud Computing is and why its so awesome lets hop back into the delorean and travel back about 20 years to a time where the internet was growing and demand for resources was increasing.

Projects required months if not years of planning of servers, these were huge financial investments which would stay with companies for years. If demand increased you'd be waiting for many months before you got your additional resources, and even when they arrived a SysOp engineer would have to drive to the data centre (where the server is installed) and spend days configuring and fitting the server. Then finally it could have the software installed and code deployed onto it.

So as you can tell this process was a long tedious process that delayed business growth and presented major risk in the event of any failures whether it's a fault with the server itself or at the data centre.
